## Additional Elements That Should Be Added
### 1. Add a short value proposition
Right now, users may see the interface before they understand what Revibe actually does.

**Add:**
- A one-line tagline under the logo
- A 1–2 sentence explanation on the welcome or onboarding screen

**Example:**
- “AI-guided recovery and safer workouts, personalized for you.”
- “Revibe builds a plan around your goals, monitors form, and helps reduce injury risk.”

---

### 2. Add a clearer bridge from goals to features
Users choose goals, but it is not fully clear what the app will do with those choices.

**Add:**
- A short “Based on your answers…” summary before the plan is generated
- 3 small benefit bullets or icons showing what the user will get

**Example:**
- Personalized exercise plan
- Real-time movement feedback
- Injury-aware recommendations

---

### 3. Add supportive microcopy
Some screens may look clean, but still feel unclear.

**Add:**
- Helper text under questions
- Small labels under buttons
- Reassurance text such as “You can change this later”

This would be especially helpful on the injury/body-area screen and the plan creation screen.

---

### 4. Add a lightweight onboarding preview
Users may trust the app more if they can quickly understand what it actually does.

**Add:**
- 2–3 simple preview cards during onboarding, such as:
  - “Track form with camera guidance”
  - “Get safer exercise suggestions”
  - “Follow a plan matched to your body and goals”

---

### 5. Add a fixed onboarding header structure
This is partly a layout issue, but also an information structure issue.

**Add:**
- A fixed top section that includes:
  - Question number
  - Progress bar
  - A short step label such as “Your Needs,” “Your Goals,” or “Your Plan”

This would make the onboarding flow feel cleaner and more organized.

---

### 6. Add stronger CTA context
“Create My Plan” is acceptable, but it may feel slightly generic.

**Add:**
- A subtitle above the button, such as “Your answers are ready”
- Or use more specific CTA text:
  - “Generate My Plan”
  - “Build My Revibe Plan”

---

### 7. Add a trust signal
Because the app is related to exercise, pain, and injury risk, users may want reassurance.

**Add:**
- “Personalized for your needs and caution areas”
- “Designed to help you move more safely”
- “Adjustable anytime as your condition changes”

---

## Overall Summary

Beyond fixing alignment and layout, Revibe should also add:
- clearer explanation of the product,
- better feature-to-benefit communication,
- supportive microcopy,
- lightweight onboarding guidance,
- and trust-building language.

The feedback suggests the UI is visually promising, but the app still needs to do a better job of helping first-time users quickly understand what Revibe is, how it works, and why they should trust it.
